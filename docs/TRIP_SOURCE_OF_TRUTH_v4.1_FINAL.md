# 0. Meaning of status labels

- **CONFIRMED** — supported by a booking confirmation or explicit user correction.
- **AGREED** — explicitly accepted in the planning discussion or present in the approved PWA plan.
- **RECOMMENDED** — newly refined logistics, route or restaurant choice; useful, but not a reservation.
- **OPTIONAL** — depends on weather, energy or queue length.
- **RECHECK** — timetable or operational detail that must be verified shortly before travel.
- **OPEN** — still genuinely unresolved; do not silently fill it.

# 1. Trip philosophy

- Travellers: Jakub and two children.
- Japan dates: **21 July–5 August 2026**.
- One clear theme per day.
- Normal start: breakfast around 08:00, departure around 09:00–09:30.
- Early starts only where they protect a reservation or create a genuinely better experience.
- Avoid the “stress version” of the journey.
- Keep space for food, rest, heat, queues and spontaneous discoveries.
- The number of attractions is less important than the quality of the shared experience.
- The itinerary intentionally favours calm, memorable family experiences over maximising the number of attractions.
- If unexpected delays, weather or fatigue occur, optional activities should be reduced before compromising confirmed reservations or increasing stress.
- Hiroshima sightseeing is deliberately omitted.
- Naoshima has three nights to create time for cycling, art and swimming.
- Tokyo is split into two different experiences:
  - first stay: modern Tokyo, Meiji Jingu, teamLab, Shibuya and Fuji;
  - final stay: Asakusa, sushi, Akihabara and departure.

## Operational morning rule — RECOMMENDED

- The normal family wake-up is **around 08:00**.
- An earlier wake-up is used only to protect a confirmed reservation, a long luggage transfer, or a genuinely time-sensitive connection.
- Morning plan times are **operational recommendations**, not bookings. They must never override confirmed times or the status of a reservation.
- A real breakfast, packing, toilet stop and a calm exit are part of every morning plan.
- Regional rail, ferry and island-bus details marked **RECHECK** remain subject to the later timetable checks in section 7.

## Taxi comfort rule — RECOMMENDED

- Taxi is the preferred option whenever it significantly shortens a journey, removes complicated transfers, makes a luggage transfer easier, or meaningfully reduces stress.
- If rail or metro is objectively faster in the actual traffic situation, use rail or metro instead.
- The purpose is not to minimise cost; it is to maximise comfort and reliability.
